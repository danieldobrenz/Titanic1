Heatmap 

plt.figure(figsize=(10,8))
sns.heatmap(train_df.corr(), annot=True, cmap='RdBu', center=0, linewidths=1);

We are going to look at how the features compare to the 'Survival' target. The 'Passenger ID' has to the lowest correlation at -0.005. That pretty much zero correlation. So we can eliminate that feature. Normally you would look for the variables that are the closest to 1 in a correlation heatmap, but remember, the target variables are 0 OR 1 so we want to just look at what is farther from 0. The 'Pclass' variable has the coefficient of -0.34, which makes sense. I would assume that the first class passengers would have a higher chance of survival. 'Age' and 'SibSp' features are further away from zero. The two biggest correlations are the 'Parch' and the 'Fare' features, which have 0.082 and 0.26.
